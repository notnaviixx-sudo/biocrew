Biological Transformation

Anaerobic digestion (AD) reduces environmental problems by turning organic waste into natural fertilizer instead of letting it pollute land, air, and water[cite: 28].

[Image of anaerobic digestion process stages]

The Output: Digestate

Digestate can be separated into solid and liquid portions[cite: 24, 25]:

  • Animal Bedding: Utilizing the solid components[cite: 26].
  • Bio-fertilizer: Rich in Nitrogen, Phosphorus, and Potassium[cite: 26, 37].
  • Soil Health: Reduces soil erosion and nutrient runoff[cite: 52].

FAQ

What causes most food waste in households?

Households discard food due to over-purchasing, improper storage, and confusion over expiration dates[cite: 21].

How does AD help the climate?

It captures methane—a gas 25-28 times more potent than CO2—and converts it into usable energy[cite: 14, 42].

Is this a long-term solution?

Yes, because organic waste is constantly generated, providing a reliable and renewable source of energy[cite: 60].
